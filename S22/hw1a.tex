\begin{enumerate}
\item Let $R$ be a ring, and $M$ be an $R$-module. The \emph{Nagata idealization} of $M$ is the ring\footnote{You can assume that this satisfies the ring axioms, but you may wish to check a couple for yourself.} $R\rtimes M$ that
\begin{itemize}
\item as an (additive) abelian group, is given by $R\rtimes M := R \oplus M$, and
\item the multiplication is $(r,m) \cdot (s,n) := (rs, rn+sm)$.
\end{itemize}
Let  $I$ be an ideal of a ring $R$ that is not finitely generated. Show that $R\subseteq R\rtimes I \subseteq R\rtimes R$, that $R\subseteq R\rtimes R$ is module-finite, but $R\subseteq R\rtimes I$ is not module-finite.

\

\item Let $K$ be a field, $x$ be an indeterminate, and $R$ be a ring such that $K \subseteq R \subseteq K[x]$. Show that $R$ is a finitely generated $K$-algebra. What if $K$ is not a field?

\

\item Find a finite generating set for $\R[x,y,z]$ as a $\R[x^2+yz,x+y,z]$-module.

\


\item Noetherian rings and prime ideals:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Show that if every prime ideal of $R$ is finitely generated, then $R$ is Noetherian.\footnote{Hint: If not, take an ideal $I$ that is maximal among the ideals that are not finitely generated, take $f,g\notin I$ with $fg\in I$, and consider the ideals $(I,f)$ and $I:f  := \{ r\in R \ | \ rf\in I\}$.}
\item If the set of prime ideals of $R$ satisfies ACC, must $R$ be Noetherian?
\end{enumerate}

\

\item Show that if $R$ is a unique factorization domain, then $R$ is integrally closed in its fraction field.

\item[(B)] Let $A\subseteq B$ be a module-finite inclusion of rings. If $B$ is Noetherian, then so is~$A$.

Take a maximal element of \[\{IB \ | \  I \ \text{is an ideal of} \ A \ \text{and} \ B/IB \ \text{is not a Noetherian $A$-module}\}\] and reduce to the case $B/aB$ is a Noetherian $A$-module for any nonzero $a\in A$. \[ \{  N \subseteq B \ | \ N \ \text{is an} \ A\text{-submodule of} \ B \ \text{and} \ B/N \ \text{has annihilator zero}\}\]
