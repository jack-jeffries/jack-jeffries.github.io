  <section class="definition" role="region" aria-labelledby="def-group">
    <h3 id="def-group">Definition (Group)</h3>
    <p>
      A <dfn>group</dfn> is a set \(G\) with a binary operation \(\cdot\) such that:
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Associativity:</strong> \((x \cdot y) \cdot z = x \cdot (y \cdot z)\).</li>
      <li><strong>Identity:</strong> There exists \(e \in G\) with \(ex = x = xe\).</li>
      <li><strong>Inverses:</strong> Each \(x \in G\) has \(y \in G\) with \(xy = e = yx\).</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section class="example" role="region" aria-labelledby="ex-gl">
    <h3 id="ex-gl">Example</h3>
    <p>
      For a positive integer \(n\),
      \[
        GL_n(\mathbb{R}) = \{ \text{invertible } n \times n \text{ matrices over } \mathbb{R} \}
      \]
      is a group under matrix multiplication.
    </p>
  </section>
